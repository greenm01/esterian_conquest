EC report cadence focus

This artifact is the compact Rust-facing evidence extract for the last timing
families that remained ambiguous after the broader `ec-report-transition-*`
work.

The important conclusion is not one more hidden delay table. The preserved
corpus instead supports:

- standing extended-orbit status carry across years
- later hostile-contact reports while orbiting as independent weekly-stream
  events
- standing bombardment continuation after hostile encounter, not one fixed
  `attacked -> bombing-run` delay

Practical meaning:

- `orbit-world` is a standing mission/status family
- week-`1` orbit reports are normal round-start carry for fleets already in
  extended orbit
- the preserved zero-gap `identified -> orbit-world` cases are therefore not
  evidence of a universal same-week delay collapse

DIRECT SAME-SOURCE / SAME-YEAR PROGRESSION

- `entered-system -> attacked`
  - count: `2`
  - gaps: `{0: 1, 1: 1}`
  - interpretation: arrival and hostile contact are separate weekly-stream
    events; attack week is not derived mechanically from entry week

- `identified -> orbit-world`
  - count: `7`
  - gaps: `{0: 5, 1: 1, 4: 1}`
  - all `0`-gap cases are week `1`
  - interpretation: standing extended-orbit status, not one fixed follow-on
    timer

- `orbit-world -> sensor-contact`
  - count: `23`
  - gaps: `{1: 2, 2: 3, 3: 4, 5: 2, 8: 2, 10: 3, 12: 1, 14: 1, 16: 1, 26: 2, 28: 1, 36: 1}`
  - interpretation: later contact while orbiting reflects independent hostile
    traffic/detection while the standing orbit status persists

- `attacked -> bombing-run`
  - count: `4`
  - gaps: `{0: 1, 5: 1, 6: 1, 7: 1}`
  - the `0`-gap case is week `1`
  - interpretation: standing bombardment continuation after hostile contact,
    not one fixed post-attack delay

- `intercepted -> bombing-run`
  - count: `1`
  - gaps: `{6: 1}`
  - interpretation: the bombardment continuation family is not tied only to
    the literal `attacked` wording; hostile encounter during standing
    bombardment is the better model

RUST-FACING CONCLUSION

These families no longer need to be modeled as unresolved delay constants.

- `entered-system -> attacked` belongs to shared-stream arrival/contact logic
- `identified -> orbit-world` and `orbit-world -> sensor-contact` belong to
  the standing extended-orbit family
- hostile encounter during bombardment can be followed by later
  `bombing-run` continuation without one single global offset
